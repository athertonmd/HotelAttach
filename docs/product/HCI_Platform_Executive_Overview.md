# HCI Compliance Analytics Platform — Executive Overview

## Platform Purpose

The HCI Compliance Analytics Platform helps Travel Management Companies (TMCs) and their corporate clients maximise hotel booking compliance, protect revenue, and fulfil duty of care obligations for business travellers.

The platform monitors every trip from the moment it is created through to departure. It identifies opportunities where hotel accommodation should be booked but has not been, recommends the right communication timing for each traveller, and provides real-time visibility into compliance performance across the entire travel programme.

## High-Level Workflow

1. **Trip Created** — A PNR arrives from the GDS or booking tool. The platform identifies whether hotel accommodation is required.

2. **Opportunity Detected** — If a hotel should be booked but has not been, an opportunity is created and scored by urgency, revenue potential, and compliance severity.

3. **Behaviour Intelligence Applied** — The platform analyses the traveller's historical booking patterns and determines the optimal communication timing and channel.

4. **Communication or Suppression** — If the traveller is likely to book independently, communication is suppressed. Otherwise, the recommended action (email, SMS, push, or agent escalation) is triggered at the predicted optimal time.

5. **Outcome Recorded** — When the traveller books, declines, or the trip expires, the platform records the outcome and uses it to improve future predictions.

6. **Analytics Reported** — All activity flows into real-time dashboards for operations teams, management, and corporate clients.

## Core Modules

### 1. Opportunity Operations

**Business function:** Identifies and prioritises hotel compliance gaps across the entire travel programme.

**What it shows:**

- Active opportunities requiring attention
- Priority classification (critical, high, medium, low)
- Opportunity type (missing hotel, orphan booking, coverage gap)
- Pipeline progression from detection through to resolution
- Estimated commission value per opportunity

**Who uses it:** Operations team leaders and compliance analysts.

---

### 2. Duty of Care

**Business function:** Ensures the TMC knows where every traveller will be staying before departure, meeting employer duty of care obligations.

**What it shows:**

- Accommodation visibility rate across the programme
- Unresolved gaps by destination
- High-risk unresolved trips (international, high-risk destinations)
- Approaching departures requiring urgent action

**Who uses it:** Duty of care officers, corporate travel managers, and compliance teams.

---

### 3. Traveller Engagement

**Business function:** Manages outbound communications to travellers and tracks their responses.

**What it shows:**

- Communications sent by channel (email, SMS, portal, agent call)
- Response rates and conversion rates
- Booking requests created from traveller responses
- Communication effectiveness by type

**Who uses it:** Operations teams managing traveller outreach.

---

### 4. Agent Escalations

**Business function:** Routes complex or time-sensitive cases to human agents when automated communication is insufficient.

**What it shows:**

- Pending escalations requiring agent action
- Escalation reasons (no response, departure imminent, high-value opportunity, executive traveller)
- Priority classification and assignment status
- Resolution tracking

**Who uses it:** Agent supervisors and senior operations staff.

---

### 5. Behaviour Intelligence

**Business function:** Predicts when each traveller is likely to book their hotel and recommends the optimal communication strategy to maximise compliance without causing irritation.

**What it shows:**

- **Traveller Archetypes** — Classifies each traveller into one of seven behavioural types (Autopilot, Responsive, Nudge Needer, Procrastinator, Reluctant, Chaotic, New Traveller) based on their booking history.

- **Communication Fatigue** — Monitors how fatigued each traveller is from communications and automatically suppresses outreach when the risk of irritation outweighs the likelihood of a positive response.

- **Revenue at Risk** — Quantifies the commission revenue at risk for each opportunity based on the traveller's attachment likelihood and booking probability.

- **Recommendation Performance** — Tracks whether the platform's action recommendations (wait, email, SMS, escalate) led to successful outcomes, continuously improving accuracy.

- **Booking Timeline** — Visualises when travellers typically book relative to departure, revealing whether communications are being sent at the right time.

**Who uses it:** TMC management, data analysts, and product teams seeking to optimise communication strategy.

## Key Business Benefits

**For the TMC:**

- Higher hotel attachment rates through intelligent, timely communication
- Reduced communication costs by suppressing unnecessary outreach
- Agent time focused on travellers who genuinely need help
- Measurable prediction accuracy that improves over time
- Differentiated service offering for corporate client retention

**For the Corporate Client:**

- Improved programme compliance without traveller irritation
- Duty of care confidence — know where your people are staying
- Visibility into booking behaviour trends across the organisation
- Reduced out-of-policy hotel spend

**For Travellers:**

- Fewer unnecessary reminders and chase communications
- Communications arrive at a time that feels relevant, not premature
- Self-sufficient travellers are left alone
- Those who need help receive it through their preferred channel
